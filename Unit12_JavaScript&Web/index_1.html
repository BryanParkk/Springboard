Sequoia (genus)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Etymology

The name "Sequoia" is derived from the name of 1Sequoyah, a Cherokee silversmith who invented the Cherokee syllabary.

Sequoia is a genus of redwood coniferous trees in the subfamily Sequoioideae of the family Cupressaceae. The only extant species of the genus is Sequoia sempervirens in the Northern California coastal forests ecoregion of Northern California and Southwestern Oregon in the United States.[1][2] The two other genera in the subfamily Sequoioideae, Sequoiadendron and Metasequoia, are closely related to Sequoia. It includes the tallest trees, as well as the heaviest 1/8, in the world.
